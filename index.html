REPUBLIC ACT 10630

Republic Act No. 10630, also known as the "Act Establishing a Comprehensive Juvenile Justice and Welfare System," is a law in the Philippines that aims to promote the rights and welfare of children in conflict with the law. It provides a framework for the treatment and rehabilitation of children involved in criminal activities, emphasizing restorative justice principles rather than punitive measures.

RA 10630 introduced significant changes to the juvenile justice system in the Philippines. It raises the minimum age of criminal responsibility from 9 to 15 years old, recognizing that children below this age lack the discernment to fully comprehend the consequences of their actions. The law also strengthens the role of the local social welfare and development office in handling juvenile cases and creating a community-based rehabilitation program.

Additionally, RA 10630 emphasizes diversion programs and alternative measures for children in conflict with the law, such as counseling, rehabilitation, and reintegration into society. It promotes the principle of restorative justice, focusing on the well-being and best interests of the child offender. Overall, RA 10630 aims to provide better protection and support for children who commit offenses, acknowledging their vulnerabilities and encouraging their reformation, rehabilitation, and reintegration into society.

Additionally, Section 11. Section 57 of Republic Act No. 9344 is hereby amended to read as follows: "SEC. 57. Status Offenses. – Any conduct not considered an offense or not penalized if committed by an adult shall not be considered an offense and shall not be punished if committed by a child." "SEC. 57-A. Violations of Local Ordinances. – Ordinances enacted by local governments concerning juvenile status offenses such as, but not limited to, curfew violations, truancy, parental disobedience, anti-smoking and anti-drinking laws, as well as light offenses and misdemeanors against public order or safety such as, but not limited to, disorderly conduct, public scandal, harassment, drunkenness, public intoxication, criminal nuisance, vandalism, gambling, mendicancy, littering, public urination, and trespassing, shall be for the protection of children. No penalty shall be imposed on children for said violations, and they shall instead be brought to their residence or to any barangay official at the barangay hall to be released to the custody of their parents. Appropriate intervention programs shall be provided for in such ordinances. The child shall also be recorded as a ‘child at risk’ and not as a ‘child in conflict with the law’. The ordinance shall also provide for intervention programs, such as counseling, attendance in group activities for children, and for the parents, attendance in parenting education seminars."
